S1

Safety Gate

Should this provider be in the marketplace at all?
Pre-check · Not scored
Business logic
  • Query each NPI against NPDB, state medical boards, and the PECOS/OIG exclusion list
  • If any source returns an active sanction, unresolved adverse action, or exclusion → provider fails
  • Not a score. Binary check only. No weighting, no partial credit
  • A provider who fails is not listed and receives no composite quality score
  • Refresh cadence matters: Hecht v. Cigna settled for $5.7M because provider data went stale. We need monitoring, not just a one-time check

National Practitioner Data Bank

Operated by HRSA · Federal adverse action repository
Restricted access

The government's central repository for adverse actions against healthcare providers. The only place where malpractice settlements are aggregated nationally. A provider could have paid out multiple claims across different states and nothing would show on any single state board.

What gets reported: malpractice payments, adverse licensure actions (revocations, suspensions, restrictions), clinical privilege actions, DEA actions.

Out of scope for this sprint. NPDB access requires resolving a registration question that could take weeks to months. Three paths documented below for when we return to it.

Data source 2: OIG LEIE Done
2

OIG Exclusion List (LEIE)

List of Excluded Individuals/Entities · HHS Office of Inspector General
Free · Public

Every individual or entity excluded from participating in federal healthcare programs (Medicare, Medicaid, CHIP). If a provider is on this list, any entity that pays them for federally reimbursable services faces civil monetary penalties.

AccessFormatRefreshCost
exclusions.oig.hhs.govCSV bulk downloadMonthly full + supplementFree
What we found

The file has 82,749 banned providers. Our job is to check whether any provider joining our marketplace appears on this list. The problem: each provider on our marketplace has an NPI (their unique ID number), but only 10% of LEIE records have an NPI. The other 90% are from before 2007 when the NPI system started.

What those old records DO have: the person's last name, first name, and date of birth. "John Smith" alone would match too many people. But "John Smith born April 15, 1965" is almost certainly one specific person. That's why we built two matching tiers:

Tier 1
NPI match (high confidence): exact NPI lookup. Covers 8,306 exclusions (10%). No ambiguity, auto-fail.
Tier 2
Name + date of birth match (medium confidence): catches the 70,543 older exclusions that have no NPI (89%). Near-deterministic, but can miss if a provider changed their name. Flagged for review, not auto-fail.
Combined coverage: 98.9% of individual exclusions on the list. Only 865 records uncoverable (businesses without individual names, or missing DOB).

What this requires from the onboarding form: NPI alone gets Tier 1 only. To run both tiers, the provider onboarding profile must also collect legal first name, legal last name, and date of birth. Without DOB, we can only check against 10% of the exclusion list.

Validation: 10 NPI test cases caught, 5 name+DOB test cases caught, fabricated names correctly passed. Logic confirmed end-to-end.

Data source 3: PECOS Done
3

PECOS — Provider Enrollment, Chain and Ownership System

CMS · Medicare enrollment registry
Free · Public extract

CMS's enrollment system for providers who bill Medicare. Active enrollment is a baseline signal. A revocation is serious: CMS revokes for felony convictions, license loss, OIG exclusion, or billing abuse.

AccessFormatRefreshCost
data.cms.govCSV bulk downloadMonthlyFree
What we found

The file has 2,957,262 enrollment rows covering 2,542,195 unique providers. This is essentially every provider who bills Medicare in the US. We use it as a cross-reference: if a provider is actively enrolled in PECOS, it's a signal they're known to CMS and in good standing.

We tested 200 NPIs pulled from PECOS against the gate. All 200 passed (100% PASS rate), which is what we'd expect for actively enrolled providers.

Design decision: PECOS is informational, not a gate blocker. A provider not found in PECOS isn't necessarily a problem. They might just not bill Medicare (cash-pay practices, commercial insurance only, etc.). We flag the absence for context, but we don't fail a provider on it. The LEIE exclusion check is what drives the pass/fail decision.

50 states + DC · License status & disciplinary actions
Varies by state

License status, disciplinary actions, consent orders, and practice restrictions. No single national database. Each of 50 states runs its own board with its own data infrastructure.
